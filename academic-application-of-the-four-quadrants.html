8.1 Academic Application of the Four Quadrants

Freudian Psychology is an example of a subjective analysis of the workings of the human mind, so this belongs in the Upper Left Quadrant.

Behavioral Psychology, as developed by B.F. Skinner, looks at the workings of the human mind from an objective perspective. So this field of study belongs in the Upper Right Quadrant.

Many elements of cultural studies provide examples of an intersubjective view, from the interior of a social collective. So these perspectives belong in the Lower Left Quadrant.

Sociology is an example of an objective view of society, so this belongs in the Lower Right Quadrant.

An understanding of these four quadrants of perception is useful because it helps us understand that these very different views of reality are not contradictory or mutually exclusive, but rather are complementary. Views from all of these different perspectives may be equally valid, even if they are very different ways of looking at the world.
