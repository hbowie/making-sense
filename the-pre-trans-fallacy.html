11 The Pre/Trans Fallacy

When we examine the two ends of the Developmental Levels – starting wth the Archaic Level and ending with the Holistic Level – we can see that both ends are, to some degree, nonrational, with rationality becoming dominant in the middle, in the Modern Level.

Ken Wilber distinguishes between these two ends of the spectrum, with the beige beginnings being termed pre-rational and the turquoise at the top being called transrational. The pre-rational state simply has not yet achieved any degree of rationality, while the transrational state has included and integrated the rational levels, but achieves some degree of transendence over them.

Wilber noticed that many people conflated these two very different states, resulting in either a romantic idealization of the pre-rational, or a mistaken condemnation of the transrational as reversion to an earlier state of development. This confusion he termed the “pre/trans fallacy.”
